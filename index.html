About

Chanel’s Cause was set up by 8 year old Chanel Murrish and her family - brothers Cole age 9, Chase age 11 and Mam and Dad(Fay and Micheal Murrish).

Chanel was diagnosed antenatally with an extremely rare heart condition known as Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome with Intact Atrial Septum. To date has undergone 24 operations and procedures, including 4 open heart surgeries. A cardiac arrest / stroke at 17 days old resulting in cerebral palsy and epilepsy.

The aim of Chanel’s Cause is to give back to the Hospitals and Charity’s that help families on such journeys as their own. Aswell as supporting others families with children in similar life threatening situations.

Chanel's Cause has picked up numerous awards over the years for its fundraising efforts. Local and national - from Northeast Charity Award, Sunderland Echo and Newcastle Chronicle Awards, Pride Of Britain Regional (Northeast Award). To BHF Award and Chanel being the youngest person to be honoured with letters after her name at Parliment, Chanel Murrish BCyA, from the British Citizen Youth Awards. Her ground breaking first open heart surgery and world class medical teams skill also seen Chanel entered into The British Medical Journal and the Guiness World Records as the Youngest Open Heart Surgery Patient. Her nomination to the very first The Suns Heath Awards also saw her amazing cardiac consultant pick up 'Best Doctor'.

Over the years Chanel's Cause has raised over £10,000 to date for CHUF, The children’s Heart Unit Fund at the Freeman Hospital specialist unit in Newcastle. Providing lifesaving equipment for children born with complex congenital heart defects or requiring transplants. Funds were raised through numerous ways. Chanel took part in a charity catwalk, only weeks after learning to walk with help from a leg splint after suffering a stroke - but has sold SO many charity bracelets and even managed to grab the attention of some celebrities to help. She and her brothers have took part in numerous sponsored walks over the years and even managed to get local schools to hold ‘wear red days’ to help raise funds. In addition funds have been raised through Christmas card sales and so much more! The Cause also has had numerous participants take part in the Greta North Run each year.

Chanel has also helped raised into the hundreds of thousands of pounds and rising for the British Heart Foundation. She has travelled to London numerous times for photo shoots and filming and featured in many of their campaigns. She has a brilliant relationship with the BHF team and shares her story whenever she can to help both raise funds and awareness. For extra fundraising sales from BHF calendars has been sent to the charity for multiple years to support The British Heart Foundation in their incredible work and research into heart conditions.

Chanel's Cause has had amazing help over the years from local buisness's in raising funds to support other charities and their Christmas Campaign. From charity buckets in numerous places to venues holding charity event nights.

Every Christmas Eve for the last 8 years, Chanel's Cause has fundraised separately on top of everything else to raise enough to personally take in gifts to children and families in hospital too poorly to make it home over the festive season, as well as NHS who are working over it. The family delivers gifts, hampers, food, mascots and so much more in the hope of bringing some extra Christmas magic to the wards on a special time for all but still a difficult time for so many.

Chanels journey has been shared through social media since she was born (please see social media links at the bottom of the webpage), now she is older she regularly helps update it herself. She also speaks to local and national media regularly, raising much needed awareness of heart conditions especially in children and young people. Hoping to raise awareness and impact on the importance of organ donation too.

Finally, a support group on social media was also set up in 2014, the Heart Family Group. With the aim to connect other families with similar conditions for support. They also try to maintain yearly meet ups in person which can be beneficial to both the parents and children affected by CHD's.
